# Code of Conduct

MacCleaner is an open community. We want it to stay welcoming, useful, and free of drama. This document sets out the behaviour we expect from everyone who participates — contributors, maintainers, and people just opening their first issue.

## What we expect

- **Be helpful.** Most people opening an issue or PR are stuck or excited about something. Treat them that way.
- **Be specific.** Vague feedback ("this sucks") helps no one. Concrete feedback ("this crashes when I do X with Y selected") helps everyone.
- **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements are misunderstandings. Ask before you escalate.
- **Credit work.** Reviewers credit authors. Maintainers credit reporters. Code reuse credits its source.
- **Stay on topic.** Issues are for the project. Personal politics, recruiting, marketing, and unrelated tech debates belong elsewhere.

## What we don't accept

- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained mockery directed at another participant.
- Harassment of any kind, including unwanted contact after someone has asked you to stop.
- Slurs, demeaning stereotypes, or content that targets people for who they are.
- Sharing someone's private information without permission.
- Spam, off-topic promotion, or repeated bad-faith argumentation.
- Threats — explicit or implied, online or off.

## Scope

This applies anywhere the project lives: issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, chat channels, and any in-person events that happen under the MacCleaner banner. It also applies to off-platform behaviour that materially affects the project's community.

## Reporting

If you see a problem, or are the target of one, email **princeks@blendapp.ae** with:

- What happened (links / screenshots help)
- Who was involved
- Whether you've already tried resolving it directly

You'll get an acknowledgement within 72 hours. Reports stay confidential to the people handling them. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is itself a violation.

## How we respond

Maintainers will weigh severity, intent, and pattern. Possible responses, roughly in order:

1. **Private nudge** — a quick "hey, please don't do that here."
2. **Public correction** — an on-thread reminder of the rules.
3. **Edit or remove the offending content.**
4. **Temporary cooldown** from project spaces (a few days to a few weeks).
5. **Permanent ban** from project spaces.

We aim for proportionality. First-time slip-ups usually get a nudge. Sustained or severe behaviour skips ahead.

## Maintainers

Maintainers are held to the same rules as everyone else. Reports involving a maintainer should be sent to a different maintainer, or to the project owner directly.
